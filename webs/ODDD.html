A Dyeing Clustering Algorithm based on Ant Colony Path-finding Mechanism

Abstract

While maintaining the purity of data, Outlier Detection (OD) can also have a certain mining ability for unknown objects, which has been fully applied and studied in various industries. Aiming at the problem of insufficient accuracy of traditional detection methods, an Outlier Detection algorithm based on Dynamic Displacement (ODDD) is proposed. Through the attraction rules established in the iterative framework, the point set is driven to move towards the target direction so as to accumulate and mine abnormal features of data. Firstly, a basic measurement model of data energy is proposed to provide data support for attracting correlation. Then, a kind of bidirectional attraction relation is established to realize the target movement of points and realize the accumulation of anomalies. Furthermore, in order to find the optimal separation boundary of the two kinds of data, a disturbance detection technique and evaluation method are proposed, which can search for the best distribution and return scores in the process of attraction. Finally, in order to visualize the results, a decision graph is created to effectively show the cluster centers and outliers. The algorithm is verified on 3 synthetic datasets and 5 real high-dimensional datasets. Moreover, 10 classical and famous OD algorithms are compared among the 4 indicators, and the results show that the proposed method has higher accuracy and effect.
